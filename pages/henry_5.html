Henry V

  1. St. Crispian's Day.

    2. "This day is called the feast of Crispian He that outlives this day, and comes safe home Will stand a tip-toe when the day is named, And rouse him at the name of Crispian.

    He that shall live this day, and see old age, Will yearly on the vigil feast his neighbors, And say 'To-morrow is Saint Crispian' Then will he strip his sleeve and show his scars. And say 'These wounds I had on Crispin's day".

  2. Band Of Brother

    3. "We few, we happy few, we band of brothers; For he to-day that sheds his blood with me Shall be my brother; be he ne'er so vile, This day shall gentle his condition."
